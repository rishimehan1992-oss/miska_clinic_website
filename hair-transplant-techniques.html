Follicular Unit Extraction (FUE)

FUE is the most popular hair transplant technique worldwide. It involves extracting individual follicular units directly from the donor area (typically the back and sides of the scalp) using a micro-punch tool (0.7â€“1.0mm diameter). Each graft is removed one at a time, leaving tiny dot-like scars that are virtually invisible even with short hair.

How FUE Works

The surgeon uses a motorised or manual punch to create circular incisions around each follicle. The graft is then gently extracted and collected. Recipient sites are created in the balding area, and grafts are placed at the correct angle and direction for natural growth. The entire procedure is performed under local anaesthesia.

Direct Hair Implantation (DHI)

DHI uses a specialised pen-like instrument called the Choi implanter. Unlike FUE, where extraction and implantation are separate steps, DHI allows the surgeon to extract and implant in one motion. This reduces the time grafts spend outside the body, potentially improving survival rates.

How DHI Works

The Choi implanter has a hollow needle that holds the graft. The surgeon inserts the needle into the recipient site and releases the graft in one step. This allows precise control over angle, depth, and direction â€” critical for a natural hairline. No pre-made incisions are needed; the implanter creates the channel as it goes.

Advantages of DHI

  • Minimal graft handling â€” Reduces trauma and improves viability
  • Precise placement â€” Excellent for natural hairlines and density
  • No pre-made slits â€” Faster healing, less bleeding
  • Higher density possible â€” Grafts can be placed closer together

Considerations

DHI typically costs more than standard FUE. Procedure time can be longer for large sessions. Requires specialised training. Ideal for patients seeking the most natural hairline and density.

FUT, also known as the strip method, was the dominant technique before FUE. A thin strip of scalp (usually 1â€“2 cm wide and 15â€“25 cm long) is removed from the donor area. The strip is then dissected under a microscope into individual follicular units, which are transplanted into the recipient area. The donor wound is closed with stitches.

How FUT Works

After local anaesthesia, the surgeon removes a strip of scalp from the back of the head. The strip is processed in a lab to separate follicles. Tiny incisions are made in the recipient area, and grafts are placed. The donor incision is stitched; the scar is hidden by surrounding hair but visible if you shave very short.

Robotic Hair Transplantation

Robotic systems such as ARTAS use advanced imaging and algorithms to identify and harvest the best follicles. The robot assists with extraction, ensuring consistent punch angle and depth. Some systems also aid in recipient site creation.

How Robotic Hair Transplant Works

A computer maps the donor area and identifies optimal follicles. The robotic arm positions the extraction tool with high precision. The surgeon oversees the process and performs implantation. The technology reduces human fatigue and variability.

Advantages

  • High precision and consistency
  • Reduced human error
  • Minimally invasive, FUE-style result
